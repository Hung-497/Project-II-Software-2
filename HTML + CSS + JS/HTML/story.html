Will it ever be the same again?
It might have been the 16th. Or the 18th. You aren’t sure anymore.Days don’t separate themselves the way they used to , maybe they never did.
You woke to a letter you don’t remember receiving.No sender. Just fragments. Scratched-out lines. A warning. The airport looked normal enough at first. People walking. Coffee machines buzzing.But something felt missing. The sky was white. Not cloudy, not bright.White like an unfinished page.
Conversations sounded simpler than they should.Clothes looked wrong, like someone forgot half the details. Simpler. Easier.Wrong.
You decide you need answers.And whatever the world has become - whatever is still disappearing - the map is where you start.
