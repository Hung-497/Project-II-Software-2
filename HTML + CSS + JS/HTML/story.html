The Story of Survival Flight

Amazing news! A second layer to the microwave background radiation was discovered. Scientists came to the conclusion that it’s a new form of a black hole (red hole, or the hole of death - they call it). To be more precise, it is a wormhole, an unfortunate portal to The Database. Our world has taken a long time to program. So long, that not even our minds on the verge of complete insanity can comprehend. However minds are not completely useless, they hold small bits of information about reality, which makes it easier to handle the information on The Database. It stores all the data that is holding our realities together, holding, and creating, space for the old and new worlds. The Red Death is gigantic, but invisible. Its vendetta doesn’t make you shiver or panic, it lullabies you till you are peacefully, eternally, sleeping. It manifests itself in simpler and simpler language with which you hear your neighbours spreading rumours, in a more and more uncomplicated clothing and style, in a naive mind. The Red Death is little by little erasing everything that distinguishes us from a vacuum, swallowing the code of which our world consists. A requiem to humans written in real time. As the civilisation of the human race evolved, so did the science. Their search for meaning, or more precisely, illusion of its abundance for their minuscule, insignificant time. Petty try to make themselves useful. And to only drive themselves only more to the corner, they got to the technological revolution. The revolution consisted of creating a new civilisation resembling them. However, the revolution didn’t go as planned. One of the beta-technicians working on the infrastructure got into the system that handled The Database. Access to any Database handling unit was heavily controlled. One of the programs in the Database was civilians’ obedience, so the control was depended on that, no physical force was ever used whatsoever. Nevertheless, this one beta-technician was a bug in the code. The structure of The Database wasn’t as complicated to understand, but more so to handle. By mistake, or not, the technician managed to delete one of the core parts of the program. The people couldn’t feel it, but the program that they consisted of was slowly destroying itself. Like an undefined variable called in a function, the world’s marrow began to melt away. Starting from the stuff we don’t seem to notice, the change is so simple yet so stupefying that it is even funny to question. So, naturally, nobody does. After some time the Red Death sucked most of the computers out of existence. People went missing, most touching memories forgotten. Only a couple of The Database maintenance computers are left semi-destroyed in the world. On these computers, only a few programs remaining. And somewhere, far away from the scientists and their headlines, an engineer wakes up on a bench. Cogito, ergo sum. Thank Database I am finally awake. I cannot say how long I’ve been sleeping, but it sure was a good sleep for an engineer like me. As the feeling comes back to my limbs, I can feel something quite sharp under me. Sitting up, which proves to be difficult, I start to see blurry surroundings. Red, white, black - a banner, a wall, my shoes. Oh goodie, how did they end up so far away? As I’m trying to remember what happened, my head starts throbbing. There are cases when you enjoy your pain. Well, that wasn’t one of these cases. I could feel metallic spikes growing from my bones, piercing the shape of meat that I am. White, white, white. All is white and screaming, screaming from my ears, since I cannot move my mouth. I drop on my knees, oh what a dumb idea it was to stand anyway. After a while, when, at least, the screaming, my own internal screaming, has quieted, I tried again. Standing on my legs was bizarrely unfamiliar, and it took a while to get used to it. Walking to my shoes, I notice something dropping from my pocket. The first and last sentences are encrypted. The only thing in plain text reads something like “there are still computers left, scattered around the EU that can help you”. When putting on my shoes, it hits me. Jesus encryption. Of course. The letter uses one of the best encryption techniques of our time. There is a lot to unpack here and only few answers. Walking around, I felt proud of myself for figuring out that the place is an airport, since I remember airports looking differently. They usually were not these huge white sterile-looking blocks. But I can feel the atmosphere of rushing to finally see the loved ones, to get to an important meeting on time, of last-minute coffee before the flight. Life is so meaningful when you just look it in the eyes. I walk to a flock of people who gathered near a stand. Something red catches my attention - a magazine. It depicts a red hole - is this a joke? I, as little as I know about cosmology, know that the holes that are in spaces are black or white. As I’m trying to concentrate on the far-away magazine, someone spills his coffee and curses. When I look back I realise that the magazine’s cover reads “The Favourite Roses of the Season”. My mind must not be fully woken up hallucinating this much. Arriving to the gathering, I see a gelato stand. Ah, so we’re in Italy! Bongiorno, my teasing treat. Paying for the gelato, I can sense something off. The seller is looking at me too intently. Lorenzo, that was his name, made me wait a bit longer than the others. When finally he did return, he gave me a napkin with barely intelligible writing scribbled on it. “5”, under which “Gelato can taste sweet.”. What is this, a joke? 5 gelatos can taste sweet huh? I go to grab a coffee from a coffee shop to balance the sweetness of my vanilla ice cream. It takes me a little bit of time to realise that most like the number is related to the letter that I found in my pants. Who does this person think they are? Either something really weird is happening, or someone has to put their creativity into a better use. While the hot cup is burning my hand, I realise that the napkin might be a hint. Some high tech engineers do that sometimes, they make others’ life more difficult by not saying the thing straight and think they’re so cool. Anyway, I concluded the sentence to be an ICAO code. GCTS. Pack your shorts and sunglasses. But of course, since there is nothing for me to pack, I just buy the ticket with the money I will find in my pocket. Wow, I must have been really rich. After traveling for around two weeks, solving riddles and whatnot, I finally have most of the letter completed. Looking back, I can’t imagine how I did not just collapse from all the thinking and walking I have done. There were some computers, which of I somehow know something - I may have been a programmer in a different lifetime, that decoded parts of the messages. They were so bare, I didn’t see other stuff in them but just the deciphering programs. After a time I figured out that the hints were pretty straightforward - I always tried to explore most of the airports, since I had the time. The second airport I went to a bookstore owner gave me a book, with the title “Les Fleurs du Petit Garçon”. In the same place I found a small computer with a raspberry on it. Then I went to about ten airports with no luck. After nearly giving up, I went to a coffee shop in the French Charlie and found a chipmunk with “Eheh :)”. Great, now they’re just laughing at my stupidity. Looking back I just don’t get it how I didn’t see it. The ICAO code was right in front of my eyes without any hints and puzzles and I chose to ignore it. Well, in Eindhoven I saw such a beautifully simple sunset. With a warm tea and a spicy cinnamon bun in my in hand it was one of those moments which you look back to with a warm tear on your face, especially when you understand that it was one of the last agreeable moments you had. In the lounge I saw an arrow to the left, where I found yet another computer. No wonder nobody used, and just ignored them - they were blank without any fun programs in them. I managed to decrypt yet another part of the message, but then the computer displayed “ERROR 504: Data packet lost Driver mismatch detected Database access denied File structure corrupted” - glitch in the system, but maybe another hint? In Frankfurt I got the last part of the message. The computer was hidden in plain sight. Just there, standing in the middle of the airport. It sounded like the German people were speaking about something important, but it is difficult to understand when all you know is “hände hoch”. I decoded the last part of the letter and felt nothing. All the traveling, all the crash outs and beautiful moments for nothing. “(1)You have a great change to save the world, (2) do what you can, (the first/ original text) there are still computers left, scattered around the EU that can help you (3) to remember who you are (4) but don’t get too excited, (5) since I tricked you; the secret base, the key, my password - remember?”. And I did, as a tsunami focused on its vendetta the memories crashed into me like a personal attack. Like a personal attack for all the sorry mistakes I’ve made in my trivial life. In ym trivial life, affecting everyone, forever. I was pushed back into the regretful day I wanted to know. I wanted to know something that wasn’t meant for me for a reason. The legend about The Database, The Technicians. Terry, who wrote me the letter. He only wanted to laugh at me? He went extinct at some time, don’t exactly remember when. But even after that, my mistake was not enough, he wanted me to remember it forever. Oh Terry, I thought I fooled you by taking your logging in as you. By looking through the code in your name. By pretending to be you. But you were someone else all along. It was me who made the error. Because of me the people and the my most beloved world is slowly dying. After so much time being in nervous stupor, after so many hours of thinking so much that it wouldn’t have been weird if I went insane, I started to feel a sensation of peace. The peace came from forgetting what is difficult to digest. The code is still erasing itself, but this time, it was me who is destroying the synapse connections which make up the last couple of weeks’ events. Destroying myself, there is only one thought that pops into my mind - “winning is understanding that there are some battles that can’t be won no matter how hard you try”. Other than that it is just peace. I feel myself drifting away and choose to lie down. It has been a long battle for nothing, and I deserve to lie down on this bench. Cogito, ergo sum. But maybe that is the problem.
